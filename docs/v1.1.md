The current implementation is now a clean non-OLG baseline with Swedish-
style labour-tax scenarios, revenue-neutral calibration, inequality
diagnostics, mobility diagnostics, output saving, and plots. Compared with
the intended architecture, the main gap has shifted: the tax/diagnostic
side is now fairly developed, while the transfer, calibration,
behavioural, and OLG layers are still missing.

Implemented Now

- Fixed-population non-OLG ABM.
- Skewed initial wealth distribution.
- Persistent labour income.
- Persistent heterogeneous capital-return classes.
- Wealth accumulation with saving and after-tax capital returns.
- Flat tax and Swedish-style labour-income tax scenarios:
    - low progressivity
    - baseline progressivity
    - high progressivity
- Revenue-neutral first-year labour-tax calibration.
- Lump-sum redistribution.
- Wealth inequality metrics:
    - wealth Gini
    - top 10% share
    - top 1% share
    - Lorenz curves
- Income diagnostics:
    - pre-tax labour-income Gini
    - disposable-income Gini
    - capital-income Gini
    - capital-income share
    - average tax rate by income decile
- Mobility diagnostics:
    - wealth-quintile transition matrix
    - Shorrocks index
    - rank correlation
    - top 20% persistence
    - bottom 40% to top 40% movement
- Scenario runner with CSV outputs, final summary, mobility summary, and
plots.

Still Missing From Intended Architecture

- No Swedish-style transfer system yet.
Current transfers are only equal lump-sum redistribution. Missing:
    - universal transfer component
    - social insurance
    - means-tested safety floor
    - public investment / mobility channel
- No unemployment or social insurance shocks.
Labour income is persistent, but there are no unemployment spells or
replacement benefits.
- No age structure.
Missing:
    - age-income profile
    - retirement
    - pensions
    - death
    - lifecycle dynamics
- No OLG or inheritance.
Missing:
    - cohorts
    - parent-child links
    - inheritance
    - offspring replacement
    - intergenerational mobility metrics
- No historical Swedish calibration.
Missing calibration to:
    - Swedish wealth Gini
    - top wealth shares
    - income distribution
    - tax burden
    - capital-income share
    - Pareto tail behaviour
- Saving behaviour is still simple.
Current saving rates are random and fixed. The architecture suggests
saving rates may depend on income or wealth rank.
- Capital returns are bounded and heterogeneous, but not wealth-dependent.
This matches the recommended first implementation, but the architecture
suggests later robustness checks with wealth-dependent return
advantages.
- No HSV tax function robustness check.
The architecture recommends retaining HSV taxation as an alternative
smooth progressivity specification.
- No decomposition experiments yet.
Missing experiments such as:
    - remove transfers
    - remove capital-income tax
    - remove heterogeneous returns
    - remove public investment
    - remove inheritance
- Pareto tail index is not implemented.
Wealth Gini and top shares are implemented, but not tail-index
estimation.

Bottom Line
The current model is a strong non-OLG baseline for testing whether
revenue-neutral Swedish-style labour-income progressivity compresses
disposable income and wealth inequality.

The intended final architecture is broader. The biggest remaining steps
are:

1. Add Swedish-style transfer components.
2. Add calibration/validation against Swedish stylised facts.
3. Add richer behavioural saving rules.
4. Add robustness/decomposition experiments.
5. Later, add age, pensions, inheritance, and OLG dynamics.
